PERSONAL LOAN

A personal loan is an unsecured form of credit, meaning you don't need to pledge any collateral such as a car or property. This gives borrowers more flexibility, since the loan can be used for a wide range of purposes—such as consolidating debt, covering medical expenses, financing a major purchase, or paying for travel.

Documents required to apply for Personal Loan

  • Identity Proof - Passport, Voter's ID, Driving License, PAN Card, Aadhaar Card
  • Proof of Residence or Address Proof - Passport, Voter’s ID, Driving License, PAN Card, Aadhaar Card, Electricity Bill, Telephone Bill, Ration Card
  • Age Proof - Passport, Voter’s ID, Driving License, PAN Card, Aadhaar Card
  • Income Proof - 1 year Bank statement, 3 months Salary Slips
  • Employment Proof - Employment Certificate, Office address proof
  • GST or VAT Registration for Self-employed
  • Photograph - Passport-size photographs
  • Business proof - Business registration documents such as a partnership deed, Memorandum of Association (MOA), Articles of Association (AOA), etc.
  • Income tax returns – Documents of the past 2-3 years to verify income and tax payment history

LOAN AGAINST PROPERTY

A Loan Against Property(LAP) is essentially a type of mortgage loan, as you are required to mortgage your property to safeguard the lender against the risk of non-payment or default. Before approving the loan, the lender evaluates your personal and financial profile, including factors such as nationality, age, occupation, income, and the market value of the property being offered as collateral. A mortgage loan calculator then helps estimate the financial implications of the loan by assessing key eligibility parameters, assisting both the borrower and lender in determining the feasibility of approval.

Documents required to apply for Loan Against Property

  • Required to Apply for Loan Against Property
  • Proof of identity/residence
  • Proof of income
  • Property-related documents
  • Proof of Business (for self-employed)
  • Account statement for the last 6 months

PROJECT LOAN

A project loan is a type of financing provided specifically to fund a clearly defined project - such as construction, infrastructure, business expansion, renewable energy plants, manufacturing units, or large equipment purchases. Unlike general business loans, the loan is evaluated and repaid based on the project’s ability to generate cash flow, not just the borrower’s existing financial strength.

Documents required to apply for Project Loan

Initial Project Documents:

  • Business Plan: Outlines the project's objectives, strategies, and expected outcomes
  • Financial Models: Detailed projections of revenue, expenses, and cash flow to demonstrate the project's viability
  • Market Feasibility Study: Analysis of market conditions to support the project's potential success
  • Financial Feasibility Study: Assessment of the financial aspects to ensure the project can be funded and sustained

Legal Documents:

  • Engineering, Procurement, and Construction (EPC) Contracts: Agreements that outline the responsibilities of contractors and the terms of construction
  • Loan Agreements: Legal contracts that specify the terms and conditions of the loan, including repayment schedules and interest rates
  • Partnership Agreements: If applicable, documents that outline the roles and responsibilities of partners involved in the project

Construction-Related Documents:

  • Construction Timeline: A detailed schedule outlining the phases of construction and key milestones
  • Blueprints/Plans: Architectural designs and plans for the project, often required for permits and approvals
  • Proof of the contractor's qualifications and legal ability to perform the work

Financial Statements:

  • A summary of personal assets and liabilities, often required for individual borrowers
  • Tax Returns: Previous years' tax returns to verify income and financial stability
  • Proof of Insurance: Documentation showing that the project is insured against potential risks

Additional Documentation:

  • Authorization to Obtain Credit Information: Permission for lenders to check the borrower's credit history
  • Estimate of Construction Costs: Detailed breakdown of expected costs associated with the project
  • Zoning and Permitting Documents: Proof that the project complies with local zoning laws and has obtained necessary permits
